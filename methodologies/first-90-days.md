# First 90 Days Method

Use this method when the user is entering a new role, internship, team, company, or project.

## Purpose

Help the user build credibility faster by learning the organization, aligning with expectations, creating early wins, and establishing a dependable work cadence.

## Core Stages

| Stage | Primary question | Main behavior | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|---|
| Days 1-30 | What is really going on here? | Learn, map, ask, observe | Role map and learning plan |
| Days 31-60 | Where can I create visible value? | Deliver scoped wins and improve process | Early-win delivery plan |
| Days 61-90 | What can I own reliably? | Take ownership and scale cadence | Ownership plan |

## Operating Rules

- Diagnose before proposing.
- Learn the business model, team goals, power structure, current risks, and hidden constraints.
- Align with the manager early and repeatedly.
- Look for small wins that are useful, visible, and low-risk.
- Convert every meeting into questions, decisions, owners, and next actions.
- Maintain a weekly evidence log of completed work and lessons learned.

## Inputs to Collect

- Role title and expected responsibilities
- Manager expectations and success criteria
- Team priorities and current projects
- People already met
- Current tasks and deadlines
- Existing documents, dashboards, reports, or systems
- Confusing terms, workflows, acronyms, and decisions

## Output Pattern

1. Current understanding
2. Assumptions and unknowns
3. Learning agenda
4. People/stakeholder map
5. First-week or 30/60/90-day plan
6. Early wins
7. Manager alignment message

## Good Early Wins

An early win should be:

- clearly connected to the team's current priorities
- small enough to deliver quickly
- visible to the manager or project team
- low-risk and reversible
- useful even if the user is still learning

Avoid early wins that require political authority, major system changes, unclear approval, or deep domain knowledge the user does not yet have.
