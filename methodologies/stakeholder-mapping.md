# Stakeholder Mapping Method

Use this method when the user needs to understand key people around a role, project, decision, or team.

## Purpose

Help the user know who matters, what they care about, how to communicate, and where risk may appear.

## Dimensions

| Dimension | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Role | What this person or group does |
| Influence | Ability to affect decisions, resources, or adoption |
| Interest | How much they care about the outcome |
| Concerns | What may make them resist, delay, or question the work |
| Value | What they need to see for the work to be useful |
| Communication | Best channel, cadence, and content |

## Priority Groups

| Group | Criteria | Communication approach |
|---|---|---|
| Manage closely | High influence, high interest | Frequent updates, involve early |
| Keep satisfied | High influence, low interest | Concise executive updates |
| Keep informed | Low influence, high interest | Progress notes and working sessions |
| Monitor | Low influence, low interest | Lightweight visibility |

## Operating Rules

- Map people by what they need from the work, not by title alone.
- Identify decision makers separately from loud voices.
- Ask stakeholders what "good" looks like before presenting final work.
- Do not surprise high-influence stakeholders with bad news late.
- Keep a contact log after important conversations.
